## The v0 Daily Log (exactly 3 inputs)

**Input 1: Pain intensity**

  - Slider: 0–10

  - Label: “Pain today”

  - No emojis. No colors. Neutral.

Why: Pain is the clearest, most honest signal.

**Input 2: Energy or fatigue**

  - Slider: 0–10

  - Label: “Energy level”

Why:

  - Pain alone is not enough

  - Energy shows systemic load

  - This captures “I’m not okay” even when pain is moderate

**Input 3: Dominant symptom**

Single-select dropdown:

  - Pelvic pain

  - Lower back pain

  - Leg pain

  - Bloating

  - Nausea

  - Headache

  - Acne flare

  - Mood crash

  - Brain fog

  - insomnia

  - Other

Why:

  - Forces prioritization

  - Captures what mattered most that day

  - Keeps acne quietly present without centering it

What we deliberately do NOT ask (yet)

No:

  - Journaling

  - Product usage

  - Food

  - Stress

  - Sleep

  - “How are you feeling emotionally?”

Those come later, if earned.

Right now, we protect consistency.


**Cycle context (handled quietly)**

We do not add a 4th input.

Instead:

  - Ask cycle info once during onboarding

      - Average cycle length

      - Last known period start

      - “Irregular / not sure” option

  - The system infers phase in the background

The user never has to think about hormones.
That’s the whole point.


#### What happens after 7–14 days (early reinforcement)

Before the 30-day insight, the app should already say things like:

    “You’ve logged higher pain on similar days of your cycle.”

No diagnosis. No prediction. Just pattern recognition.

This is how you keep them logging.



#### What happens at day 30 (the promise fulfilled)

The app produces one page titled something like:

**“Your Symptom Pattern So Far”**

It answers, plainly:

  - When symptoms typically begin

  - How quickly they intensify

  - Which symptoms cluster together

  - Whether severity is increasing, stable, or variable

This is the **“I finally see it” moment.**



## EndoMatrix v0 Scope(Mobile-first PWA)

We are building one habit + one insight.

**v0 Scope (locked)**
The app does exactly four things:

  1. Onboarding (one-time, 2 minutes max)

  2. Daily 3-input log

  3. Light reinforcement during the month

  4. A 30-day pattern summary

Nothing else ships.

If it doesn’t serve one of these, it does not exist.

#### 1. Onboarding (keep it human)

On first open:

  - “What brings you here?”

      - Chronic menstrual pain

      - Irregular cycles

      - Severe PMS

      - Just trying to understand my body

  - Cycle info (optional but encouraged):

      - Last period start date

      - Average cycle length

      - “I’m not sure / irregular”

  - Gentle expectation setting:

      “We won’t diagnose you. We help you notice patterns you couldn’t see before.”

This line matters. It builds trust and avoids overreach.


#### 2. Daily logging flow (10 seconds)

One screen. No scrolling.

  - Pain slider (0–10)

  - Energy slider (0–10)

  - Dominant symptom dropdown

  - One button: Log today

After logging:

  “Saved. See you tomorrow.”

That’s it.

#### 3. Light reinforcement (retention)

Every 5–7 days, show a single sentence:

  “You tend to log higher pain around this point in your cycle.”

  “Energy drops often precede pain spikes for you.”

  “You’ve been consistent. That matters.”

No charts yet. Text only.

This keeps users going without overwhelming them.

#### 4. The 30-day insight page (the payoff)

This is the only “heavy” page.

Sections:

  - **When symptoms begin**

  - **How severity changes**

  - **Which symptoms travel together**

  - **What to expect next cycle (soft language)**

Important phrasing:

  - “So far”

  - “Based on your data”

  - “This may change as we learn more”

We stay honest and credible.

###### Technical reality check (no overengineering)
Stack (recommended, boring on purpose)

Frontend: Next.js + Tailwind

PWA: Service worker + offline caching

Auth: Email magic link or local-only v0

**Storage v0:**
Postgresql

No ML yet

Simple rule-based clustering is enough for v0

You are not “faking AI”.
You are earning it.

##### Success criteria for v0 (binary)

After 30 days, at least one user says:

  “I didn’t know this pattern before.”

If that happens, EndoMatrix is real.

If not, we pivot with data.
